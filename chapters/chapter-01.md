# Chapter 1

## The Gathering Storm

### Public Release Cut

This public version preserves a key late-chapter reveal for the private canon draft while keeping the opening hunt and atmosphere intact.

The rain slid down the bones of a dead city, turning old monuments into blurred silhouettes every time lightning split the sky. Towers that had once watched over a living civilization now stood hollow and overgrown, half-swallowed by vines and shadow. Beneath one of them, wrapped in a tattered cloak, Kaeden waited in silence with his katana within arm's reach and the storm pressing cold against his skin.

Peace never lasted long in a place like this.

A guttural cry rolled through the streets. Then came a scream somewhere in the dark, distant enough to echo, close enough to matter. Kaeden's eyes opened at once. Beneath the hood, violet irises caught the reflection of rainwater and lightning alike as he reached for the cracked device hidden in his cloak.

The display stuttered to life. His Eldrin levels were still recovering. Not enough for comfort. Not enough for mistakes. He checked the old photo still buried in the device's broken interface, let himself feel the pull of memory for only a second, then shut the feeling down and loaded the last of his stored Eldrin into his system.

When the warning finally came, it came fast.

Scraplings burst from cracks in the street and from puddles of violet ooze, all teeth, shrieks, and twitching limbs. Small on their own. Dangerous in numbers. Worse now, because they were moving with intent. Coordinated. Herded. Kaeden knew what that meant before he ever drew the blade.

He met them first with calm.

Sheathed strikes. Measured footwork. A kick that scattered bodies into one another. Then the katana came free in a bloom of violet fire, and the whole intersection became a blur of motion and heat. Scraplings burned where they fell, leaving behind smoldering residue and fragments of Eldrite that glittered in the rain.

By the time the last of them collapsed into blackened mist, Kaeden was already moving again.

The city funneled him forward through dead intersections, broken storefronts, and streets choked by rust and roots. Somewhere ahead, the thing hunting him answered the storm with sounds no living throat should have been able to make. Human weeping twisted into laughter. Beast cries braided with the shriek of bending steel. Even the buildings seemed to shudder under its approach.

Kaeden ran toward it.

He crossed the ruins with the practiced ease of someone who had spent years surviving above broken ground and beneath broken skies. For brief flashes between thunder, memories tried to surface: a brighter city, three figures at his side, laughter on rooftops, the shape of a life that had not yet been ruined. Then the noise found him again, and the memory was gone.

The junkyard waited at the end of the chase like the mouth of a trap.

Under a rusted shelter roof, Kaeden took the only pause he could afford. He sketched hurried notes into his journal, marking the Scraplings' coordinated behavior and the questions it raised. He ate standing on exhaustion alone, listening to the rain drum against metal, until weariness dragged him into a brief, uneasy sleep filled with academy halls, practice rooms, and the ghost of a weapon ceremony he had never really escaped.

When he woke, the air had changed.

The pressure in the junkyard felt almost physical now, as if the creature's presence were trying to force him to his knees before the fight had even begun. Kaeden stepped out into the rain with his katana in hand and found it waiting for him in the wreckage ahead: a shifting monstrosity of flesh, metal, bone, and hunger, too deliberate to be called mindless and too wrong to be called anything else.

The battle that followed was less a duel than a forced march toward a single narrow opening.

Kaeden gave ground when he had to, cut when he could, and bled for every second he bought. The creature adapted constantly. Armor grew where it was wounded. New movements emerged mid-fight. His Eldrin reserves fell with each clash, while the junkyard closed around them in rusted walls and hidden paths he had memorized long before the storm broke.

That was the difference between being hunted and setting the hunt.

Step by step, strike by strike, he drove the creature exactly where he wanted it.

Then the trap sprang.

Buried rods of Eldrin-forged metal erupted from the junk beneath the beast, pinning it in place just as the storm answered Kaeden's whispered command. Lightning crashed down from the heavens and chained through the junkyard in blinding arcs, flooding the night with white fire, ozone, and the sound of something powerful finally being made to suffer.

Even then, the thing refused to die quietly.

Its body convulsed, tore itself apart, and changed again inside the lightning. Flesh sloughed away. Its shape compressed. What emerged from the storm was smaller, leaner, faster, and far more desperate.

Kaeden steadied himself as his reserves bled toward empty.

The storm screamed overhead. The creature tore free. And in the space between one heartbeat and the next, hunter and hunted threw themselves toward the final clash.

The rest remains in the core draft.
